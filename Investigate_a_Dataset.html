1. Introduction¶

In this project, I explored the data regarding medical appointments in Brazil. The dataset, retrieved from Kaggle, contains information of more than 100,000 medical appointments, details about the patients, and whether they showed up for their appointments. The main aim of this project is to determine factors that could be associatied with the patients' likelihood of turning up for appointment and these are my research questions when conducting the analysis:

  1. Does age influence the show up rate?

  2. Which gender is more likely to show up for their appointments?

  3. Does being handicap influence the show up rate of patients?

  4. What impact does receiving SMS play in the likelihood of patients showing up?

  5. Are patients under the Bolsa Família program more likely to show up?

  6. Are hypertension, diabetes, or alcoholism related to the likelihood of patients showing up?

Here are the descriptions of the columns as understood from the information in the Data Dictionary section in Kaggle,

  • The PatientId column records the identification number of each patient.

  • The AppointmentID column records the identification number of each medical appointment.

  • The ScheduledDay column shows the date when the patient scheduled their appointments.

  • The AppointmentDay column shows the date of the appointments.

  • The Neighbourhood column shows the locations of the hospitals.

  • The Scholarship column implies whether the patients are enrolled in a Brazilian welfare program called 'Bolsa Família' (0 means no and 1 means yes).

  • The Hipertension, Diabetes, and Alcoholism columns indicate whether the patients have hypertension, diabetes, or alcoholism, respectively (0 means no and 1 means yes).

  • The Handcap column indicates whether the patients have any handicap. Not much is explained on what each value means, so I assume that 0 means the patients do not have handicap, and any other value means that they do.

  • The SMS_received column shows whether the patients received SMS notifications about their appointments (0 means no and 1 means yes).

  • The No-show column implies whether the patients were absent for their appointments. 'Yes' means that they were absent, while 'No' means that they were not absent. (i.e. they showed up for their appointments).

I noticed that there are two problems with the column names:

  • Firstly, there was some spelling mistakes (Hipertension and Handcap).

  • Secondly, The first two columns are not named consistently. In the first column, the term 'id' is spelled with an uppercase 'I' and a lowercase 'd', but in the second column both 'I' and 'D' are in uppercase.

So, I renamed the columns.

2.2 Data Cleaning Part I: Column Names and Data Type¶

After exploring the dataset, there are a few areas of the dataset that need to be checked and cleaned:

  • The data in the 'PatientID' column are stored using floats instead of integers.

  • The data in 'ScheduledDay' and 'AppointmentDay' columns are stored in string format instead of datetime format.

  • The appointment times are all at 00:00:00, which indicate that the patients arrange their appointments to be at 12 midnight.

This seems unusual because people probably do not go for medical appointments at that timing. With that in mind, I proceeded to clean the data

4 Conclusions¶

After conducting the analyses, I found that there is some association between patients' likelihood of showing up and these variables:

  • Age
  • Hypertension
  • Diabetes
  • Handicap type
  • Scholarship (i.e. the Bolsa Família welfare program)
  • SMS

There are also two surprising findings:

The Bolsa Família welfare program did not help to increase the show up rate. In fact, there is a lower proportion of patients enrolled in the program that showed up. Those that received SMS about their appointments are also less likely to show up.

However, these findings are only tentative. Statistical tests will be needed to explore the significance of these associations. Apart from that, the lack of information about the meaning of different values in each column also hindered the inferences that could be made during the analysis. In the Handicap column, not much detail is given on what each value means, so I made the assumption that 0 means the patients do not have handicap, and the other values refer to different types of handicap. The different values could also possibly mean levels or severity of handicap, but without more information I could not be sure. Also, in the columns that contain only values 0 and 1, such as Scholarship and Hypertension, there is no explanation of the meaning of each value, so I assumed that 0 means false/no and 1 means true/yes. If the values mean otherwise, the conclusion made from this analysis will have to be changed. Therefore, to better understand the relationship between the patients' show up rate and the other variables, statistical tests and clarification on the meaning of different values in the columns are required.
